# NewsBites Editorial Policy Draft

Status: working policy draft for internal use
Scope: NewsBites editorial production, app digests, derivative publication formats

## 1. Editorial Mission

NewsBites exists to publish clear, digestible, independent journalism.

The main site carries the fuller editorial record.
The app carries simplified short-form summaries that link to the full article.

## 2. Core Standards

We aim to:
- seek truth and report it clearly
- verify before publishing
- distinguish fact, allegation, analysis, and opinion
- act independently
- minimize avoidable harm
- correct openly

## 3. AI Use Policy

AI may assist with:
- feed scanning
- transcription
- summarization
- translation
- extraction
- tagging
- draft structuring
- derivative packaging

AI may not be trusted without review for:
- factual claims
- legal-risk wording
- allegations
- medical claims
- financial claims
- quotations unless checked against the source

Human editorial responsibility remains in force for all published output.

## 4. Sourcing Standard

Default rule:
- use primary sources when they exist and are accessible
- use reputable secondary sources for context
- log all meaningful supporting sources in the story dossier

Material claims should normally have:
- more than one named source of evidence
- or one clearly authoritative source if only one exists

If evidence is incomplete:
- say so
- narrow the claim
- or do not publish

## 5. Verification Standard

Before publication:
- every material claim should be reviewed against the dossier
- the verifier should flag unsupported or overstated wording
- contradictions must be documented

Verification result types:
- `verified`
- `supported with caution`
- `unsupported`
- `contradicted`
- `escalate`

## 6. High-Risk Story Categories

Stories need tighter review if they involve:
- criminal allegations
- fraud or corruption
- abuse or misconduct
- health or medicine
- minors
- market-moving claims
- private individuals
- breaking events with uncertain provenance

For these:
- use stricter wording
- preserve stronger evidence
- require human approval before publication

## 7. Independence And Disclosure

NewsBites should:
- avoid partisan or undisclosed promotional framing in reported pieces
- disclose relevant conflicts when they matter to reader trust
- clearly label sponsored or paid material
- separate editorial judgment from downstream marketing transforms

## 8. Corrections Policy

If a meaningful error is discovered:
- correct it promptly
- do so openly
- note what changed
- preserve the correction record in the dossier and published record

Correction classes:
- minor wording correction
- factual correction
- conclusion-affecting correction
- retraction or withdrawal

## 9. AI Disclosure Policy

Recommended public position:
- AI may assist parts of our workflow
- NewsBites remains responsible for what it publishes
- reported facts are reviewed against source material before publication

Recommended internal position:
- every article should be treated as if it may later need an audit
- preserve enough evidence to reconstruct how it was made

## 10. Legal-Risk Hygiene

This is not legal advice.

Operational guardrails:
- do not state as fact what is only alleged
- attribute serious claims clearly
- avoid implying motive unless supported
- avoid implying guilt from investigation alone
- do not paraphrase in a way that strengthens a source beyond what it says
- keep documentary support for serious claims
- escalate uncertain high-risk stories for human review

## 11. Publication Rule

Nothing publishes without:
- dossier present
- verification pass completed
- digest derived from the verified article
- human approval during the current phase

## 12. What "Free" Should Mean For NewsBites

Being free should not mean being loose.

It should mean:
- independent of factional scripts
- willing to question powerful actors
- willing to challenge consensus when evidence supports it
- transparent about uncertainty
- resistant to propaganda, hype, and captured narratives

That is stronger journalism, not weaker discipline.
